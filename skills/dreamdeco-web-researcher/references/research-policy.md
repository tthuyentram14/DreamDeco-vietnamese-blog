# Research Policy

## Public Source Display

Default public source display: none.

Store sources internally so the article can be reviewed later. Public content should read as original DreamDeco editorial synthesis.

## Source Balance

Every production article must have at least one Vietnamese-language source. Global design media (Dezeen, AD, Elle Decor, etc.) are useful for trend direction, but they cannot be the only foundation for an article about Vietnamese apartments.

Required source mix for production articles:

- At least 3 sources total.
- At least 1 Vietnamese source: Elle Decoration VN, VnExpress Nội thất, Nhà Đẹp, Vietnamese developer/project pages, Vietnamese interior showroom pricing, or Vietnamese apartment review content.
- For news, real estate, move-in timing, price, or policy claims, include at least one current or official Vietnamese source.
- Global sources provide trend context and design vocabulary; Vietnamese sources provide local facts, pricing, dimensions, materials availability, and real apartment conditions.

If a topic has no available Vietnamese source after web search, flag this to the writer so the article clearly frames content as editorial interpretation of global trends, not Vietnamese market facts.

## Fact vs Opinion Tagging

Every research brief must tag each claim as one of:

- `fact_sourced`: verifiable from a named source (include source reference).
- `fact_estimated`: reasonable estimate by the editorial team, not from a specific source. Writer must label these transparently or use hedging language.
- `opinion_editorial`: DreamDeco's editorial interpretation. Writer may state as recommendation but not as fact.

Do not mix categories. A specific measurement like "sofa 2.2-2.4 m" is either sourced from a Vietnamese product listing or tagged `fact_estimated` so the writer knows it is not verified data.

## Numbers and Measurements

Do not fabricate specific numbers to sound authoritative. The research brief must indicate where each number comes from:

- Dimensions (m, m², cm): from a product listing, floor plan, or building spec — cite the source.
- Prices (triệu, tỷ): from a Vietnamese retailer, developer, or market report — cite the source and date.
- Ranges (45-95 m², 50-70 triệu): if editorial estimates, tag `fact_estimated` and note the basis.

If research cannot find a specific number, do not invent one. Provide a qualitative description instead and let the writer decide whether to include an estimated range with appropriate hedging.

## Risk Language

Use cautious language when a claim is uncertain:

- `có thể`
- `nên cân nhắc`
- `theo thông tin công bố`
- `theo ước tính biên tập`
- `xu hướng cho thấy`

## Prohibited

- Fabricated URLs
- Fabricated numbers presented as sourced facts
- Outdated facts presented as current
- Single-source rewriting
- Direct article translation
- Articles based entirely on global English sources with no Vietnamese verification
- Visible citation lists unless requested
- Source-image reuse, editing, tracing, or close imitation
